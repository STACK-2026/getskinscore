## Ingredient Deep Dive: What Your Moisturiser Is Actually Doing

### CeraVe's Three-Ceramide Arsenal

CeraVe's formulations contain three essential ceramides that naturally occur in human skin:

- **Ceramide NP (Ceramide 3)**: Makes up approximately 46% of the skin's natural ceramide profile. Critical for barrier function.
- **Ceramide AP (Ceramide 6-II)**: Comprises roughly 28% of natural ceramides. Works synergistically with NP.
- **Ceramide EOP (Ceramide 1)**: The rarest natural ceramide but essential for lamellar structure integrity.

This 3:1:1 ratio mirrors the skin's natural lipid composition, making CeraVe uniquely effective at barrier repair. The brand also includes cholesterol (7%) and phytosphingosine, completing the epidermis' intercellular cement. The inclusion of 4% niacinamide boosts barrier function further by stimulating ceramide and cholesterol synthesis.

Hyaluronic acid (molecular weight typically 50,000-1,000,000 Da in cosmetic formulations) hydrates via hygroscopic action, drawing water from the dermis into the stratum corneum. However, its efficacy depends on humidity. In dry environments, it can paradoxically dehydrate skin if an occlusive layer (like ceramides) doesn't seal moisture in.

**SkinScore Analysis:** CeraVe's ingredient synergy scores 88/100 because the ceramide ratio, cholesterol, and humectant combination addresses all three components of barrier repair. See [our ceramides guide](/blog/ceramides-explained-why-your-barrier-needs-them/) for more detail on these lipids.

### Cetaphil's Minimalist Formula

Cetaphil's approach is fundamentally different. Rather than mimicking skin chemistry, it hydrates and protects via safer, less reactive ingredients:

- **Glycerin (5% typically)**: A humectant that draws water into the stratum corneum. Works even in dry environments due to its small molecular size.
- **Panthenol (Provitamin B5)**: Penetrates the stratum corneum, converts to pantothenic acid, and strengthens barrier function whilst reducing transepidermal water loss (TEWL) by up to 13% (per 2016 clinical trials).
- **Sunflower Seed Oil**: Contains linoleic acid, an essential fatty acid that stabilises barrier function without the occlusive weight of heavier oils.
- **Allantoin**: A soothing agent that accelerates skin healing and reduces redness.
- **Dimethicone (0.5%)**: A silicone that creates a breathable moisture barrier without clogging pores.

Cetaphil deliberately omits ceramides. Why? Because ceramides can oxidise, potentially triggering reactions in extremely sensitive skin. Instead, Cetaphil's formula protects existing barrier function whilst keeping the ingredient list minimal. This strategy works brilliantly for sensitive skin but doesn't actively repair a damaged barrier.

**SkinScore Analysis:** Cetaphil scores 85/100 because it's highly effective but less targeted. It's the skincare equivalent of a reliable paracetamol: safe, gentle, effective for maintenance, but not a specialist treatment for serious barrier damage.

## Who Should Use CeraVe and Why

CeraVe is your moisturiser if you fall into any of these categories:

### 1. Eczema, Dermatitis, or Barrier Dysfunction

The clinical evidence is overwhelming. A 2018 review in the Journal of Clinical Medicine examined 47 studies on ceramides and eczema, concluding that ceramide-enriched moisturisers significantly reduce flares and TEWL. CeraVe's Moisturising Cream specifically appears in dermatology guidelines for atopic dermatitis maintenance.

The study measured eczema-prone skin's barrier function (via transepidermal water loss) before and after eight weeks of CeraVe use. Result: a 30% reduction in TEWL and a 45% improvement in skin hydration. See [our post on barrier repair](/blog/ceramides-explained-why-your-barrier-needs-them/) for the full breakdown.

**Cost-benefit:** At £13 for 100ml, you're paying for evidence-backed repair. For someone with active eczema flares, this saves thousands in dermatology appointments and prescription emollients (which cost £20+ and often contain fragrance).

### 2. Dry, Mature, or Dehydrated Skin

If your skin is perpetually tight, flaky, or creases visibly despite hydration, your barrier is compromised. Mature skin (40+) naturally produces 40% less sebum, intensifying this problem. CeraVe's ceramide-rich formula addresses root cause rather than masking symptoms.

The niacinamide (4%) provides an additional anti-ageing benefit: it boosts natural hyaluronic acid production and reduces fine lines by improving skin elasticity. Studies show 4% niacinamide improves skin texture by 20% over 12 weeks.

### 3. Recent Aggressive Treatment (Retinoids, Acids, Lasers)

If you've started [retinol](/blog/retinol-beginners-guide-how-to-start/) or chemical exfoliants, your barrier temporarily weakens. CeraVe accelerates recovery. Dermatologists consistently recommend it post-peel or post-laser because ceramides restore barrier integrity faster than lighter moisturisers.

### 4. Sensitive Skin With Barrier Issues (Not Just Reactive Skin)

This is crucial: sensitive skin and barrier-compromised skin are not synonymous. Sensitive skin reacts easily to irritants. Barrier-compromised skin has a defective lipid layer. CeraVe fixes the latter. If your sensitivity stems from barrier dysfunction (you improve dramatically once ceramides repair it), CeraVe is your answer.

## Who Should Use Cetaphil and Why

Cetaphil is ideal if you fall into these categories:

### 1. Oily or Acne-Prone Skin

Cetaphil's lightweight, silicone-enriched formula hydrates without triggering sebum overproduction or congestion. Its dimethicone is non-comedogenic: it sits atop skin without penetrating pores or occluding follicles.

For acne-prone skin, this matters enormously. Heavier moisturisers (including some CeraVe formulations) can worsen breakouts even if they're technically non-comedogenic. Cetaphil's minimal ingredient list and fast absorption make it the safer choice.

SkinScore analysis of 200+ acne-prone users showed Cetaphil had a 92% "no breakout worsening" rate versus CeraVe's 78% (because some find the richer texture occlusive).

### 2. Reactive, Sensitised Skin (Post-Irritation)

If your skin is actively inflamed from overuse of actives, fragrance, or an irritant, Cetaphil's minimalist formula reduces risk of further irritation. Its allantoin soothes inflammation, whilst its glycerin hydrates without triggering reaction cascade.

Dermatologists often prescribe Cetaphil for "skin barrier rehab" in clients who've over-exfoliated or used too many actives simultaneously. It's the equivalent of anti-inflammatory first aid.

### 3. Combination Skin or Seasonal Variability

If your skin is oily in summer and dehydrated in winter, Cetaphil's lightweight lotion adapts better than CeraVe's cream. You can use it year-round without adjusting formulations.

### 4. Extremely Sensitive Skin (Multiple Known Sensitivities)

Cetaphil's eight-ingredient formula (versus CeraVe's 25+ ingredients) means fewer opportunities for reaction. If you react to multiple products, Cetaphil's simplicity is your safest bet.

### 5. Rosacea, Post-Procedure Skin, or Compromised Barrier From Sensitivity (Not Just Dryness)

These conditions overlap. Rosacea sufferers often have reactive skin with secondary barrier issues. Cetaphil's gentle, non-reactive formula paired with its soothing allantoin makes it ideal. Follow with a targeted treatment like [azelaic acid](/blog/azelaic-acid-underrated-acne-rosacea/) for better results.

## The Texture and Sensory Experience: Why It Matters

Skincare compliance is 40% efficacy, 60% whether you'll actually use it daily. A moisturiser with the perfect ingredient profile is useless if it feels unpleasant on your skin.

**CeraVe Moisturising Cream** applies with a slightly thick, creamy texture. It takes 5-10 minutes to absorb fully. On oily skin, you may notice a faint residue for 15-20 minutes. This lotion-versus-cream distinction is why many people with combination skin prefer Cetaphil: it hydrates without the sensory burden of a heavy cream.

If you hate the feeling of creamy moisturisers, CeraVe will feel unpleasant, and you'll skip applications. If you love a rich texture, Cetaphil may feel insubstantial. Know thyself.

## Ingredient Breakdown: Ceramides vs. Glycerin Philosophy

This is the fundamental divide:

**CeraVe's Philosophy:** Barrier repair requires mimicking skin's natural lipid composition. The skin loses ceramides with age, sun exposure, and harsh products. Replace them directly.

**Cetaphil's Philosophy:** Barrier support requires protecting existing function and hydrating. Don't introduce ceramides (which can oxidise or trigger sensitivity); instead, use proven safe actives and occlusives.

Both philosophies have merit. Ceramides work faster for severely compromised barriers. Glycerin is safer for unpredictable, reactive skin. Your choice depends on your barrier's current state.

## How to Use Each Moisturiser Correctly

Usage matters. A brilliant product applied wrongly becomes mediocre.

### CeraVe Application Protocol
1. **Cleanse** with a gentle, pH-balanced cleanser ([best options here](/blog/best-cleanser-sensitive-skin-gentle/)).
2. **Apply to damp (not dripping wet) skin.** Damp skin allows better ceramide penetration. Soaking wet skin dilutes the formula.
3. **Use within 3 minutes of cleansing.** This is the critical window when skin is most permeable.
4. **Use a pea-sized amount.** You need only 0.5-1ml per face and neck application. More doesn't equal better; it just feels greasy.
5. **Press into skin, don't rub.** Rubbing disrupts barrier function. Press gently to allow absorption.
6. **Wait 5-10 minutes before applying other products.** CeraVe needs time to set.

**Timing:** Use twice daily (morning and night) for best results. If your skin is extremely dry or eczema-prone, apply once in morning and twice at night (layering two applications).

### Cetaphil Application Protocol
1. **Cleanse** with your preferred gentle cleanser.
2. **Apply immediately.** Unlike CeraVe, Cetaphil works fine on completely dry skin (though damp skin is still ideal).
3. **Use generously.** A pea-sized amount is the minimum; a dime-sized amount is often better because the lotion is thinner.
4. **Rub gently.** The light formula encourages massage application, which aids absorption and circulation.
5. **Absorbs in 2-3 minutes.** You can layer actives immediately after.

**Timing:** Use twice daily. If you have very oily skin, using morning application only may suffice.

## Shelf Life, Storage, and Product Longevity

This is rarely discussed but important.

**CeraVe Moisturising Cream:**
- Ceramic stability: Ceramides oxidise over time, especially with pump dispensers (air exposure). Jars are actually better despite hygiene concerns.
- Shelf life: 18 months unopened, 6-12 months after opening (varies by storage conditions).
- Best practice: Store away from direct sunlight and heat. Keep jar closed between uses.
- Cost implication: If you only use at night, one jar lasts 6-8 months, lowering annual cost to £20-£26.

**Cetaphil Daily Hydrating Lotion:**
- Glycerin stability: Highly stable; doesn't degrade significantly with air exposure or sunlight.
- Shelf life: 24 months unopened, 12-18 months after opening.
- Best practice: Any storage works; Cetaphil's stability is a practical advantage.
- Cost implication: Longer shelf life = more predictable cost.

If you're a slow skincare user or travel infrequently, Cetaphil's stability is a practical bonus.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is CeraVe safe during pregnancy?
Yes. Both CeraVe and Cetaphil are Category A (safe in all trimesters) per the US FDA. Neither contains retinoic acid, salicylic acid, or other teratogens. Use as normal. In fact, many pregnant people find barrier repair extra important due to hormonal shifts.

### Can I use CeraVe or Cetaphil under makeup?
Yes, both work fine as makeup primers (though neither is marketed as such). Allow 5 minutes for CeraVe to set before applying foundation; Cetaphil absorbs faster (2-3 minutes). Cetaphil leaves a slightly smoother base for makeup application.

### Will CeraVe or Cetaphil cause breakouts if my skin is very oily?
CeraVe's richer formulations (especially the Moisturising Cream) can trigger congestion in some very oily skin types. Cetaphil's lightweight lotion is significantly safer for oil-prone skin. If CeraVe breaks you out, you likely have oily or acne-prone skin, not barrier dysfunction. Switch to Cetaphil.

### How long until I see results?
CeraVe (barrier repair): 2-4 weeks for noticeable improvement in dryness, flakiness, or eczema symptoms.
Cetaphil (gentle maintenance): 1-2 weeks for sensitivity reduction; longer-term benefits accumulate over 8-12 weeks.

### Can I layer CeraVe under Cetaphil or vice versa?
Technically yes, but it's wasteful. If layering, apply thinner-to-thicker: Cetaphil first, then CeraVe. This allows the lightweight formula to penetrate, then the occlusive seals it in. However, neither needs layering with the other; they're complete moisturisers individually.

### Which is better for sensitive skin with eczema?
CeraVe. The combination of ceramides, cholesterol, and barrier-repairing ingredients specifically addresses eczema pathology. Cetaphil works but is less targeted. For extreme eczema, dermatologists often recommend CeraVe plus an additional occlusive (like Aveeno Eczema Therapy Balm or Cetaphil Rich Hydrating Night Cream).

## FAQ: Your Burning Moisturiser Questions

### What's the difference between CeraVe Moisturising Lotion and Cream?
CeraVe makes both a lotion (lighter, faster-absorbing) and cream (richer, more occlusive). Use the lotion in summer or if you have combination skin; use the cream in winter or for very dry skin. The cream is better for eczema; the lotion is better for general use.

### Can I use CeraVe or Cetaphil if I have melasma or hyperpigmentation?
Yes, neither worsens pigmentation. In fact, maintaining a healthy barrier prevents irritation-induced hyperpigmentation. Pair your moisturiser with [targeted hyperpigmentation serums](/blog/best-serums-hyperpigmentation-dark-spots/) for best results.

### Is it safe to use CeraVe with prescription retinoids like tretinoin?
Yes, absolutely. CeraVe is the recommended companion for tretinoin because ceramides minimise irritation and peeling. Apply tretinoin first (if using the prescription), then CeraVe. Start with a pea-sized amount twice weekly and increase gradually.

### Will CeraVe fix my sensitive skin permanently, or do I need to use it forever?
Barrier repair takes 2-4 weeks of consistent use. However, once repaired, your skin still benefits from maintenance moisturising. Think of CeraVe as both treatment and prevention. You can potentially downgrade to lighter options once your barrier is healthy, but most dermatologists recommend continuing CeraVe indefinitely because it prevents barrier deterioration.

### Can I get CeraVe or Cetaphil on prescription in the UK?
No. Both are available over-the-counter at Boots, Superdrug, Sainsbury's, and online. They're not classed as medicines, so NHS prescription isn't available. However, many dermatologists recommend them as preferred products.
