**COSRX vs Beauty of Joseon** is the friendliest comparison in K-beauty. Both brands are affordable, transparent and gentle. COSRX is built on functional hero molecules like snail mucin and betaine salicylate. Beauty of Joseon is built on hanbang classics like rice water, propolis, ginseng and a very well made mineral sunscreen. Neither brand is trying to overwhelm you, and the right pick depends on the job you want the routine to do.

> **TL;DR**: Pick **COSRX** if you want short, functional formulas with real actives, a great gentle BHA and the reference snail mucin essence. Pick **Beauty of Joseon** if you want soothing, antioxidant rich essences, glow finishes and one of the best cheap mineral SPFs on the market. Both brands sit under 25 EUR for most products. COSRX has more targeted acids and pimple care. Beauty of Joseon has better sun protection and a warmer, more sensorial feel. Neither is a compromise. Many routines end up using both, and that is often the smartest answer.

If you have looked at any K-beauty starter list in the last three years, the same two brands show up first. COSRX went global on snail mucin. Beauty of Joseon rode the second wave with rice water, propolis and a mineral sunscreen that quietly became a cult favourite. Both are cheap. Both are transparent. Both are gentle. The question is not which brand is objectively better, it is which one fits how you actually shop for skincare. This guide compares them on philosophy, hero ingredients, sunscreen, actives, texture, price and beginner fit, with peer reviewed sources where the science matters. Every product mentioned is graded on [SkinScore](/rankings).

## COSRX vs Beauty of Joseon: two philosophies from the same country

COSRX launched in South Korea in 2013 with a deliberately unglamorous pitch. Do one job per product, keep the ingredient list short, and price it honestly. The name mixes "cosmetics" and "Rx" and signals a functional, almost clinical positioning. That focus is the reason the brand crossed over globally faster than most of its K-beauty peers.

Beauty of Joseon launched in 2010 but did not go global until the late 2010s. Its identity is hanbang, the Korean tradition of using herbal and grain based ingredients such as rice, ginseng, propolis and honey. The formulas are still short and modern, but the story is different. Where COSRX sells the molecule, Beauty of Joseon sells a soothing, antioxidant rich ritual with roots in traditional recipes reworked with contemporary cosmetic science.

That single difference explains the shape of each brand today. COSRX leans on targeted actives and pimple care. Beauty of Joseon leans on hydration, glow and daily sun protection. Both keep INCI lists honest, and if you are new to reading those lists, our [how to read an INCI list guide](/blog/how-to-read-inci-list-skincare/) is a five minute primer that makes both brands easier to shop.

## Hero ingredients: snail mucin versus rice water

The COSRX hero is the [Advanced Snail 96 Mucin Power Essence](https://www.cosrx.com/products/advanced-snail-96-mucin-power-essence), a lightweight essence at 96 percent snail secretion filtrate. Snail mucin is a humectant that also contains allantoin, glycolic acid at low doses, glycoproteins and peptides. Independent reviews indexed on [PubMed find snail secretion filtrate has moisturising and wound healing activity in vitro and in small human trials](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23724245/), and it is well tolerated on sensitive and post barrier repair skin.

The Beauty of Joseon hero is the [Glow Deep Serum with rice and alpha arbutin](https://beautyofjoseon.com/products/glow-deep-serum-rice-alpha-arbutin), a rice ferment based serum with 2 percent alpha arbutin for pigmentation. Fermented rice water is rich in amino acids and antioxidants and has been used in East Asian skincare for centuries. Alpha arbutin is a well studied hydroquinone alternative for pigment control, endorsed as a safe cosmetic ingredient by regulatory reviews such as those from the European Scientific Committee on Consumer Safety and referenced in [American Academy of Dermatology hyperpigmentation guidance](https://www.aad.org/public/diseases/a-z/hyperpigmentation-treatment).

The heroes reveal the split. COSRX gives you a hydration and repair essence built around one animal derived humectant. Beauty of Joseon gives you a glow serum built around a grain ferment and a targeted pigment active. Both work. They just answer different questions.

## Sunscreen: where Beauty of Joseon pulls clearly ahead

Sun protection is the one category where Beauty of Joseon is not just competitive but genuinely category leading. The [Relief Sun rice and probiotics SPF 50+ PA++++](https://beautyofjoseon.com/products/relief-sun-rice-probiotic-spf50-pa) is a chemical filter sunscreen with a light, invisible finish that many reviewers rank as one of the best cheap daily SPFs in the world. The mineral counterpart, the Matte Sun Stick, is also solid for oily and combination skin.

COSRX makes sunscreens, including a mineral zinc oxide option, but none of them reach the same reputation as the Relief Sun. If you are shopping K-beauty specifically to solve daily sun protection, Beauty of Joseon wins this round without much debate. Sunscreen adherence is the single biggest driver of long term skin outcomes, and a formula you enjoy applying every morning is the one that actually works. The [American Academy of Dermatology recommends broad spectrum SPF 30 or higher daily for skin cancer and photoageing prevention](https://www.aad.org/public/everyday-care/sun-protection/sunscreen-patients/sunscreen-faqs), and a light, non stinging chemical formula fits into most routines more consistently than a heavier mineral alternative.

## Actives: COSRX owns the gentle acid category

If your concern is congestion, blackheads or mild acne, COSRX has the deeper active range. The [COSRX BHA Blackhead Power Liquid](https://www.cosrx.com/products/bha-blackhead-power-liquid) with 4 percent betaine salicylate is the reference gentle BHA in K-beauty. Betaine salicylate is a milder oil soluble exfoliant than pure salicylic acid, which is why the liquid can be used more often than a stronger Western BHA without triggering the barrier. The AHA 7 Whitehead Power Liquid at 7 percent glycolic acid rounds out the exfoliant lineup, and the Acne Pimple Master Patches are a hydrocolloid staple.

Beauty of Joseon is deliberately quieter on strong actives. The exception is alpha arbutin in the Glow Deep Serum for pigmentation, and low dose glycolic acid tucked into some ferment based formulas. The brand puts most of its firepower into antioxidants and hydration rather than exfoliation or spot treatments. If you want targeted acid work, buy COSRX. If you want antioxidant plus hydration with a light pigment nudge, buy Beauty of Joseon. ## Hydration and barrier: both are strong, in different ways

COSRX hydration relies on humectants and centella. The [Centella line](https://www.cosrx.com/collections/centella) uses Centella asiatica extract, one of the most studied plant based soothing ingredients in dermatology, backed by [peer reviewed evidence for wound healing and anti inflammatory activity](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/24660073/). The Snail 92 All In One Cream is the richer occlusive sibling of the essence and works as a light night cream on most skin types.

Beauty of Joseon hydration relies on propolis, rice bran and ginseng. The [Dynasty Cream](https://beautyofjoseon.com/products/dynasty-cream) is the brand's flagship moisturiser and pairs peptides, rice bran and ginseng in a warm, satiny finish that flatters dehydrated and mature skin. The Ginseng Essence Water is a first step liquid that works like a hydrating toner. Neither brand loads formulas with heavy silicones or fragrance, which keeps both routines gentle on reactive skin. ## Texture, scent and sensorial feel

COSRX formulas are minimalist. Textures are functional rather than luxurious. The snail essence has a slightly bouncy, mucilaginous slip, the BHA liquid feels like water, and most creams are unscented. That functional feel is part of the appeal, but it does not flatter people who care about sensory experience.

Beauty of Joseon is warmer. The Glow Deep Serum has a gentle rice water scent, the Ginseng Essence has an herbal aroma, and the Dynasty Cream has a satiny, cushioned finish. Fragrance is low but present in most formulas, sourced mostly from the botanical ingredients themselves rather than added synthetic perfume. If added fragrance is a hard no for your skin, favour COSRX. If a subtle herbal profile is fine or preferred, Beauty of Joseon reads as more indulgent for the same money.

## Price: both are cheap, and neither is a dupe of the other

Most COSRX products sit between 8 and 20 EUR. Most Beauty of Joseon products sit between 10 and 25 EUR, with the sunscreens under 20 EUR. On a like for like basis the brands are close in price, with COSRX slightly cheaper on entry level items and Beauty of Joseon slightly pricier on hero serums.

Neither brand is a dupe of the other, which is a common misconception. They cover different jobs. Buying both is normal, and a routine that pairs a COSRX BHA with a Beauty of Joseon serum and sunscreen is one of the most common recommendations in the K-beauty community for good reason. The [French health authority HAS](https://www.has-sante.fr/) has issued guidance emphasising that no single cosmetic product can address every concern and that layered, complementary routines work better than one hero product used alone.

## Beginner friendliness: both win, by different paths

Beauty of Joseon is the easier brand to start with because the risk of a beginner mistake is lower. There are fewer strong actives to accidentally stack, the sunscreen is best in class, and the sensory profile makes the routine pleasant to keep doing. A beginner who buys the Relief Sun, the Ginseng Essence Water and the Dynasty Cream has a very defensible three step routine on day one.

COSRX is beginner friendly in a different way. The formulas are so simple and so cheap that you can experiment without financial risk. A newcomer can try the snail essence, the centella toner and the BHA liquid for less than the price of one department store serum. The one caution is stacking. It is easy with COSRX to grab both the BHA and the AHA liquids and use them on the same night. Do not. Introduce actives one at a time, wait several weeks between additions, and the barrier stays intact. ## A sample routine that uses both brands

The most efficient K-beauty routine for many people uses both brands together rather than picking a side. In the morning, cleanse with a gentle cleanser, apply Beauty of Joseon Ginseng Essence Water as a hydrating toner, follow with a light moisturiser, and finish with Beauty of Joseon Relief Sun SPF 50+. Sunscreen adherence is the single biggest driver of long term skin outcomes.

In the evening, cleanse, apply COSRX Advanced Snail 96 Mucin Power Essence to hydrate and soothe, use the COSRX BHA Blackhead Power Liquid on congested areas two to three nights a week, and layer either the COSRX Snail 92 Cream or the Beauty of Joseon Dynasty Cream depending on how rich you want the finish. That single routine uses each brand for what it does best and costs less than 100 EUR to assemble. ## Frequently asked questions

### Is COSRX or Beauty of Joseon better for beginners?

Beauty of Joseon is slightly easier because the sensory profile is warmer, the sunscreen is best in class, and the actives are gentler. COSRX is also beginner friendly but has more targeted acids that can trip up a newcomer if stacked. Start with Beauty of Joseon for daily basics, add COSRX for pimple care and BHA, and introduce actives one at a time.

### Which brand has better sunscreen?

Beauty of Joseon wins the sunscreen category clearly. The Relief Sun rice and probiotics SPF 50+ is widely regarded as one of the best cheap daily sunscreens on the market, and the mineral Matte Sun Stick is also solid. COSRX makes sunscreens but none of them have the same reputation or cosmetic elegance.

### Can I use COSRX and Beauty of Joseon together in the same routine?

Yes. The formulas are compatible and many popular K-beauty routines mix both brands. A common pattern is Beauty of Joseon for essence, hydration and sunscreen, and COSRX for BHA, snail mucin and pimple patches. There is no ingredient conflict between the two lineups.

### Is snail mucin safe for sensitive or acne prone skin?

Snail secretion filtrate is generally very well tolerated. It is a humectant with soothing and mild wound healing activity, and peer reviewed reviews on PubMed find no significant safety concerns for cosmetic use. Vegan users may prefer to avoid it on principle, in which case rice ferment based products from Beauty of Joseon are a plant based alternative.

### Which brand is cheaper overall?

The two brands are close in price. COSRX is usually a euro or two cheaper on entry level items like the snail essence and centella toner. Beauty of Joseon is slightly pricier on hero serums but competitive on sunscreens and moisturisers. Neither brand is significantly more expensive than the other on a full basket basis.

### Do both brands avoid fragrance and essential oils?

COSRX is mostly fragrance free and unscented. Beauty of Joseon has low but present natural fragrance in many formulas, mostly sourced from the botanical ingredients themselves. If added fragrance is a hard exclusion for your skin, COSRX is the safer default. If a subtle herbal profile is acceptable, Beauty of Joseon reads as more sensorial.

## The bottom line

COSRX is a functional K-beauty brand built on short ingredient lists and a few gentle hero actives. Beauty of Joseon is a modern hanbang brand built on rice, ginseng, propolis and a category leading mineral leaning sunscreen line. The two are not rivals in the way marketing sometimes suggests, they are complementary. Buy COSRX for the BHA and snail essence. Buy Beauty of Joseon for the sunscreen, the ginseng essence and the dynasty cream. The most efficient routine uses both.

Whichever way you split it, track how your skin responds so you know which specific product actually helped rather than which one your feed told you to buy.
