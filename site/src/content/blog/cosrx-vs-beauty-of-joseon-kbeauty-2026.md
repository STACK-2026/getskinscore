If you have ever built a Korean routine from scratch, two names appear in almost every basket: **Cosrx** and **Beauty of Joseon**. Both Korean, both under 25 US dollars per product, both cult-loved. But they solve different problems and reward different skin types. **Cosrx vs Beauty of Joseon** is not a fight about which brand is better, it is a decision about which set of ingredients your skin needs, and how much active is actually in the bottle you buy on [SkinScore](/rankings).

> **TL;DR**: **Cosrx** is the acne, oil control and barrier repair brand, built on **snail secretion filtrate** (96 percent in the Advanced Snail 96 Mucin Power Essence), low-pH salicylic acid and centella. **Beauty of Joseon** is the glow and sunscreen brand, built on **propolis** (60 percent in the Glow Serum), organic rice extract and ginseng. Cosrx wins on oily, acne-prone or reactive skin. Beauty of Joseon wins on dry to normal skin, on sunscreen and on radiance. Both are honestly dosed and neither is more "clinical" than the other.

Guidance from the [American Academy of Dermatology](https://www.aad.org/) is clear that concentration and formulation matter more than a marketing story, and peer-reviewed evidence on [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/) supports several of the ingredients both brands lean on. This guide compares them the way a dermatologist would, one active at a time.

## Cosrx vs Beauty of Joseon: the verdict at a glance

- **Choose Cosrx if** your main concerns are acne, blackheads, oily zones, redness or a compromised barrier. Cosrx dominates the low-pH acid, snail mucin and centella niche, under 20 US dollars per hero.
- **Choose Beauty of Joseon if** your main concerns are dullness, dryness, uneven tone or you want an excellent daily sunscreen. It is the strongest K-beauty brand for glow, comfortable hydration and one of the most highly rated Asian sunscreens.
- **Buy both if** you have combination skin. The brands overlap only lightly, and a Cosrx BHA plus a Beauty of Joseon sunscreen is one of the best value pairings in K-beauty.

## Who owns which corner of K-beauty

**Cosrx** launched in 2013 with a dermatology-first design: minimal fragrance, low-pH cleansers, clear active percentages on the front of the box. Their heroes cluster around three actives: snail secretion filtrate, salicylic acid derivatives and centella asiatica. If you have seen a K-beauty acne routine recommended online, Cosrx is almost certainly in it. Their broader lineup is covered in our [Cosrx guide](/blog/cosrx-guide-best-products-kbeauty/).

**Beauty of Joseon**, often shortened to BoJ, launched in 2010 and built its identity on traditional Korean botanicals: rice, ginseng, propolis, mugwort and honey. Their heroes are the **Glow Serum: Propolis + Niacinamide**, the **Revive Serum: Ginseng + Snail Mucin**, the **Radiance Cleansing Balm** with rice bran oil, and the **Relief Sun: Rice + Probiotics SPF50+ PA++++**, one of the most recommended Asian sunscreens in the world.

The overlap between the two is small. Cosrx does one propolis serum (the Propolis Light Ampule at 72 percent), Beauty of Joseon does one snail mucin product. Beyond that, they are complementary.

## Snail secretion filtrate: does it actually do anything

The formal name is **snail secretion filtrate (SSF)**, a filtered mucus from the garden snail *Cornu aspersum*. Chemically it is a mix of glycoproteins, hyaluronic acid, glycolic acid, allantoin, copper peptides and antimicrobial peptides. Every one of those is well studied individually.

Peer-reviewed evidence indexed on [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/) supports three actions for SSF:

- **Wound healing and repair.** Clinical work on Secretion of *Cryptomphalus aspersa*, a chemical relative of the K-beauty SSF, shows accelerated re-epithelialisation and reduced erythema after ablative laser and radiofrequency.
- **Antioxidant defence** from flavonoid and phenolic content.
- **Modest improvement in fine wrinkles, roughness and pigmentation** in a 12-week randomised trial versus vehicle.

The Cosrx **Advanced Snail 96 Mucin Power Essence** contains 96 percent SSF, at the high end of what appears in commercial products. Beauty of Joseon's Revive Serum uses a lower percentage combined with ginseng, aimed at anti-fatigue rather than pure repair. On evidence per bottle, Cosrx wins the snail category cleanly.

## Propolis: the anti-inflammatory bee resin

**Propolis** is a resinous substance bees collect from tree buds. Chemically it is dominated by flavonoids, caffeic acid phenethyl ester (CAPE) and terpenes. It is one of the few "natural" K-beauty ingredients with a real evidence base.

Published work on [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/) documents propolis as:

- **Antibacterial** against *Cutibacterium acnes*, the bacterium in inflammatory acne.
- **Anti-inflammatory**, inhibiting NF-kB signalling and pro-inflammatory cytokines.
- **Wound-healing**, with small trials showing faster closure of minor wounds versus standard care.

The Beauty of Joseon **Glow Serum** delivers 60 percent propolis plus 2 percent niacinamide. That is a clinically meaningful dose of both, and the niacinamide sits within the documented 2 to 5 percent range covered in the [niacinamide safety guide](/blog/is-niacinamide-safe-dermatology-research/). Cosrx's Propolis Light Ampule at 72 percent is higher in propolis but with fewer supporting actives. Winner: depends on whether you want propolis alone (Cosrx) or a glow-forward combination (Beauty of Joseon).

## Rice extract and ginseng: the traditional Korean botanicals

Rice extracts sound like a marketing story. They are not. Fermented rice water and rice bran extract contain **gamma oryzanol**, **ferulic acid**, **inositol** and phytic acid. Ferulic acid alone has an established antioxidant role and is a common companion in vitamin C serums, as we detail in the [vitamin C serum guide](/blog/vitamin-c-serum-guide-forms-concentrations/).

Small clinical studies on rice-based cosmetics report improved barrier function, mild brightening after 8 to 12 weeks, and very low irritation.

**Beauty of Joseon Relief Sun: Rice + Probiotics SPF50+ PA++++** is the flagship rice product. The rice extract is not the SPF work, that is the chemical filter blend, but it adds antioxidant support and gives the sunscreen its comfortable, glowy finish. It features regularly in our [best sunscreen for oily skin roundup](/blog/best-sunscreen-oily-skin-no-white-cast/).

Ginseng has smaller but real evidence for antioxidant activity and mild collagen support in vitro. It is unlikely to be the driver of visible change, but it is a legitimate supportive ingredient. Cosrx does not really compete in the rice or ginseng category.

## Which one wins for acne

Acne is Cosrx's home turf. Their toolkit is unusually well designed for a mass-market brand: **Low pH Good Morning Gel Cleanser** at pH around 5, the **BHA Blackhead Power Liquid** at 4 percent betaine salicylate (mechanism explained in the [salicylic acid vs benzoyl peroxide guide](/blog/salicylic-acid-vs-benzoyl-peroxide-acne/)), the Advanced Snail 96 essence for barrier recovery, and the **Centella Blemish Ampule** for redness.

Beauty of Joseon's contribution to acne is more indirect. The Glow Serum's propolis and niacinamide combination has genuine anti-inflammatory value on mild inflammatory papules, and Relief Sun is one of the few Asian sunscreens most acne-prone testers tolerate. But for stepwise, evidence-based acne care, Cosrx wins on formulation depth.

## Which one wins for glow and dry skin

Glow, hydration and the dewy K-beauty finish are Beauty of Joseon's home turf. The Glow Serum, Radiance Cleansing Balm and Dynasty Cream deliver cushioning textures on dry to normal skin, and the Ginseng Cleansing Oil is one of the best value cleansing oils in K-beauty. It pairs cleanly with the layering rules explained in our [K-beauty routine explained guide](/blog/k-beauty-routine-explained-science/).

Cosrx competes on hydration through the snail essence and the Hyaluronic Acid Intensive Cream, but its texture range is drier overall. For a plush, dewy finish, Beauty of Joseon wins.

## Fragrance, irritation and sensitive skin

**Cosrx** favours near-fragrance-free formulations, especially in the acid, snail and centella lines. The [European Academy of Dermatology and Venereology](https://eadv.org/) consistently flags added fragrance as a leading avoidable irritant, so Cosrx's approach aligns with expert guidance.

**Beauty of Joseon** is more sensorial and does use light fragrance or essential oil traces in some products, though usually below levels that cause reactions. Reactive skin should still check the INCI list, especially on ginseng and rice creams. For genuinely sensitive skin, Cosrx is the safer first pick, and its centella range is one of the best over-the-counter options for redness-prone faces, alongside the actives we cover in the [centella asiatica guide](/blog/centella-asiatica-cica-guide-complet/).

## Who should buy Cosrx

**Cosrx makes sense if** you have oily, combination, acne-prone or reactive skin, you want the highest snail mucin dose for the money, and you value near-fragrance-free formulas. It is also the strongest K-beauty brand for daily chemical exfoliation via its low-pH BHA. For a functional, dermatology-friendly K-beauty starter set, Cosrx is usually the safer first purchase.

**Cosrx is the wrong choice if** you want a plush, dewy sensorial routine, if your main concern is dullness rather than blemishes, or if you specifically want a top-tier daily sunscreen. Cosrx's sunscreens are competent but not category-leading.

## Who should buy Beauty of Joseon

**Beauty of Joseon makes sense if** you have dry to normal skin, your main concern is dullness or comfort, and you want one of the highest-rated daily sunscreens in the world at a mid-range price. It is also the strongest K-beauty brand for double cleansing formats and a warm, glow-forward finish that plays well under makeup.

**Beauty of Joseon is the wrong choice if** you have oily, congested, actively breaking-out or fragrance-reactive skin. Cosrx's clinical minimalism fits better there. It is also less well suited if you want a leave-on chemical exfoliant, since Beauty of Joseon does not currently compete in that category.

## The honest K-beauty verdict

Cosrx and Beauty of Joseon are not rivals so much as neighbours. Cosrx is the clinical, minimalist wing, focused on acne, oil, redness and barrier repair. Beauty of Joseon is the sensorial, botanical wing, focused on hydration, glow and sunscreen quality. The dermatological data behind their headline actives is real, not marketing.

If you can only buy from one, follow your dominant concern. If you can buy from both, the strongest pairing is a Cosrx BHA plus a Cosrx snail essence for treatment and repair, layered with a Beauty of Joseon Glow Serum and Relief Sun for hydration and protection. The full order of operations is covered in the [skincare routine order guide](/blog/skincare-routine-order-morning-night/). That combination costs less than a single mid-range Western serum, and it uses more evidence-backed actives.

## FAQ

**Is Cosrx or Beauty of Joseon better for beginners?**
Cosrx is usually the safer first pick because its formulations are simpler, less fragranced and more forgiving. The Low pH Good Morning Cleanser and Advanced Snail 96 Mucin Power Essence are two of the most reliable K-beauty introductions on the market. Beauty of Joseon is excellent, but its serums and creams are richer and more sensorial, which suits skin that already tolerates layered routines. If your skin is reactive or you have never used a K-beauty product, start with Cosrx.

**Does snail mucin really work or is it a gimmick?**
Snail secretion filtrate is not a gimmick. Peer-reviewed dermatology research indexed on PubMed shows it supports wound healing after ablative procedures, provides antioxidant activity, and improves fine wrinkles, roughness and pigmentation over 12 weeks of daily use. Effect sizes are modest but consistent. Cosrx's 96 percent Advanced Snail 96 Mucin Power Essence is one of the highest-dose commercial vehicles for this active.

**Which Beauty of Joseon sunscreen is the best?**
The **Relief Sun: Rice + Probiotics SPF50+ PA++++** is the flagship and the one most dermatology-focused reviewers recommend. It delivers PA++++ UVA protection, an SPF of 50 and a comfortable, non-white-cast finish that most skin types tolerate.

**Can I use Cosrx and Beauty of Joseon together?**
Yes. The overlap is small, so combining them is easy. A common evidence-based routine is Cosrx Low pH Good Morning Cleanser, Cosrx BHA on alternate evenings, Beauty of Joseon Glow Serum, Cosrx snail essence, a moisturiser from either brand, and Beauty of Joseon Relief Sun in the morning. Only avoid layering the Cosrx BHA and the Glow Serum on the same evening, because propolis and salicylic acid together can be drying for some skin types.

**Which brand is better for anti-ageing?**
Neither brand is primarily anti-ageing. The American Academy of Dermatology consistently ranks daily sunscreen and a retinoid above any single K-beauty active. Within K-beauty, Cosrx's snail essence has the clearer published wrinkle and pigmentation evidence, but Beauty of Joseon's Relief Sun is one of the most effective anti-ageing purchases you can make in either brand, because sunscreen prevents the damage the other actives try to reverse.

**Are Cosrx or Beauty of Joseon safe during pregnancy?**
Most products are pregnancy-friendly, but individual items may contain actives commonly avoided in pregnancy such as high-percentage BHA and certain botanical extracts. Cosrx's BHA Blackhead Power Liquid is usually flagged for review, and Beauty of Joseon's Ginseng Cleansing Oil contains essential oil traces. Always cross-check with a pregnancy-focused resource and, if in doubt, consult a dermatologist.

## Conclusion

Cosrx vs Beauty of Joseon is not a contest between a serious brand and a marketing brand. Both are serious. Cosrx built its identity on dermatology-adjacent minimalism, snail mucin, low-pH acids and centella, and it dominates the acne, oil and barrier corner. Beauty of Joseon built its identity on traditional Korean botanicals, propolis, rice and ginseng, and it owns the glow, hydration and sunscreen corner. Both cost a fraction of what Western prestige asks for the same molecule. Pick by your dominant concern, and if you can, pick one hero from each. That is the honest K-beauty answer.
