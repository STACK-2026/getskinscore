**Cosrx vs Beauty of Joseon**: Cosrx wins on short, functional formulas built around a few hero actives like snail mucin and betaine salicylate, which suits people who want to target one job per bottle at a low price. Beauty of Joseon wins on hanbang botanicals like propolis, rice extract and ginseng, plus a sunscreen lineup that most Western brands still cannot match. Both are transparent, affordable and cruelty-free in most markets. The deciding factor is whether you want clinical minimalism or a slightly more curated K-beauty experience.

> **TL;DR**: Pick **Cosrx** if you want targeted actives with almost no fragrance, minimalist ingredient decks and the lowest possible price per hero molecule. Pick **Beauty of Joseon** if you want hanbang-inspired formulas built on propolis, rice, ginseng and mugwort, plus some of the best affordable Korean sunscreens on the market. Both brands publish full INCI lists, both sit under 25 EUR for most products and both are widely available in Europe and the US. Many people already mix the two: Cosrx for treatment steps, Beauty of Joseon for hydration and sun protection.

Cosrx and Beauty of Joseon are the two Korean brands most likely to be recommended in a beginner K-beauty thread in 2026, and they are recommended for different reasons. Cosrx is the pragmatic, almost clinical brand that made a global name out of a single snail essence and a 4% BHA. Beauty of Joseon is the softer, more polished brand that turned traditional Korean herbal medicine, called hanbang, into a modern skincare identity built around propolis, rice and ginseng. This guide compares hero actives, prices, transparency and best picks. Every product is graded on [SkinScore](/rankings).

## Cosrx vs Beauty of Joseon: two K-beauty philosophies

Cosrx launched in 2013 with a deliberately unglamorous pitch: one job per product, short ingredient list, honest pricing. The name is a contraction of cosmetics and Rx, which signals the functional, quasi-clinical positioning. Most Cosrx bottles put the hero ingredient at the top of the INCI list and stop shortly after. The result is a range that feels engineered rather than curated, and that is exactly why beginners keep discovering it: what you see on the label is what does the work.

Beauty of Joseon launched in 2010 with a very different pitch. The brand borrows from hanbang, the Korean herbal medicine tradition, and builds most formulas around rice bran water, propolis, ginseng root extract and mugwort. Prices stay accessible and INCI lists are transparent, but the sensory experience feels closer to a slow ritual than a chemistry set.

That positioning gap explains almost everything downstream. Cosrx is the brand you buy to fix a specific concern quickly and cheaply. Beauty of Joseon is the brand you buy when you want your routine to feel like something. For the wider logic underneath both, our [K-beauty routine explained guide](/blog/k-beauty-routine-explained-science/) covers the shared logic.

## Cosrx signature actives: snail mucin, BHA, centella

Cosrx builds nearly its entire range around four molecules, and understanding them is the fastest way to shop the brand without wasting money. **Snail secretion filtrate** is the ingredient that made Cosrx famous. It is a complex humectant that holds water at the skin surface and contains glycoproteins, natural hyaluronic acid and trace glycolic acid. The peer-reviewed literature indexed on [PubMed on snail mucin and skin](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=snail+mucin+skin) supports it as a hydrating and film-forming agent with a modest wound-healing signal.

**Betaine salicylate** is the second hero. It is a gentler cousin of salicylic acid used in the [Cosrx BHA Blackhead Power Liquid at 4%](https://www.cosrx.com/products/bha-blackhead-power-liquid), which most dermatologists still list as one of the better affordable BHA options for oily and congested skin. The [American Academy of Dermatology guidance on acne](https://www.aad.org/public/diseases/acne/skin-care/tips) recognizes salicylic acid as a first-line over-the-counter option for unclogging pores, and the Cosrx formula sits well within that use case without the sting profile of a stronger 2% pure salicylic acid.

**Centella asiatica** and **propolis** round out the range in the Cosrx Advanced Snail line and the newer Centella lineup. Centella is a soothing botanical with a small but real evidence base for redness and reactive skin, which our [complete centella guide](/blog/centella-asiatica-cica-guide-complet/) covers in more detail. The overall Cosrx catalog is deeper than most people realize; for a full ranked walkthrough of which products actually deserve the hype, see our [Cosrx best products guide](/blog/cosrx-guide-best-products-kbeauty/).

## Beauty of Joseon signature actives: propolis, rice, ginseng, mugwort

Beauty of Joseon builds around four different pillars, and each one maps to a fairly specific job. **Propolis extract** is the brand's most famous ingredient, used at high concentrations in the Glow Serum Propolis and Niacinamide. Propolis is a resinous substance bees collect from tree buds; it has humectant, antioxidant and antimicrobial properties documented in [peer-reviewed reviews on PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=propolis+skin), and it pairs naturally with niacinamide for oily and blemish-prone skin.

**Rice bran water and rice extract** power the Glow Deep Serum and the Radiance Cleansing Balm. Rice ferment and rice bran are East Asian staples used for soft brightening and moisturising. The evidence base is smaller than for niacinamide, but the ingredient is well tolerated and rarely triggers reactions.

**Ginseng root extract** and **mugwort (Artemisia)** appear across the anti-aging and calming lines. Ginseng contains ginsenosides with antioxidant activity; mugwort is a soothing botanical popular in Korean formulations for reactive skin. Neither replaces clinically dosed retinoids or vitamin C, but they build a gentle routine most people tolerate without irritation. That is the point of the brand.

## Pricing: both cheap, one is cheaper per active

Cosrx is usually a touch cheaper per active than Beauty of Joseon, but the gap is smaller than most people expect. A typical Cosrx serum or essence sits between 12 and 22 EUR, while a comparable Beauty of Joseon serum lands between 15 and 25 EUR. On like-for-like buys, a full Cosrx routine of cleanser, BHA, essence and moisturiser will usually undercut a Beauty of Joseon basket by around 15 to 20 percent.

The caveat is that cheapest per bottle is not always cheapest per outcome. Beauty of Joseon's more layered formulas often mean you buy fewer products because each one feels more complete. Cosrx's minimalism can push you into buying five products where two Beauty of Joseon items would have done the job. Whichever you pick, introduce one product every two to three weeks, as covered in our [hyaluronic acid mistakes guide](/blog/hyaluronic-acid-mistakes-everyone-makes/).

## Ingredient transparency: both strong, one is more clinical

Both brands publish full INCI lists and both name their headline actives on the front label, which is the transparency baseline any credible K-beauty brand should meet. Where they differ is in tone.

Cosrx prints the exact concentration of its hero active on the front: Snail 96 essence, BHA 4%, AHA 7%, Advanced Snail 92. That precision makes it easy to plan around known percentages and avoid stacking the same molecule twice.

Beauty of Joseon leans more on the botanical story than the exact percentage. Glow Serum Propolis and Niacinamide tells you what is inside but not how much of each. Full INCI on the back closes the gap, but the front label is less clinical. Our primer on [how to read an INCI list](/blog/how-to-read-inci-list-skincare/) makes both brands easier to decode.

## The best hero products from each brand

If you buy only one product from Cosrx, it should probably be the [Advanced Snail 96 Mucin Power Essence](https://www.cosrx.com/products/advanced-snail-96-mucin-power-essence). It is a 96% snail secretion filtrate humectant that adds real hydration without a heavy occlusive feel, and it layers under almost anything. The [BHA Blackhead Power Liquid](https://www.cosrx.com/products/bha-blackhead-power-liquid) is the second-best buy for oily and congested skin, and the Centella lineup fills the gap for reactive or redness-prone skin.

If you buy only one product from Beauty of Joseon, the [Glow Serum Propolis and Niacinamide](https://beautyofjoseon.com/products/glow-serum-propolis-niacinamide) is usually the best entry point. It is a lightweight, slightly tacky serum that pairs propolis with 2% niacinamide, and it slots easily into most routines. The Relief Sun Rice and Probiotics SPF 50 and the Dynasty Cream (rice and macadamia) are the other two products that show up in almost every credible K-beauty shortlist for 2026.

## Which skin type fits which brand

Both brands are safe defaults for most skin types, but there are real matches worth naming.

Oily and acne-prone skin usually gets more out of Cosrx first. The 4% BHA liquid, the light snail essence and the propolis line all suit a shine-heavy, congestion-prone routine, and none of them carry the fragrance load that often causes issues on acne-prone faces. Beauty of Joseon has good options here too, especially the Glow Serum, but Cosrx's targeted BHA is the single strongest tool either brand offers for this concern.

Dry and dehydrated skin usually gets more out of Beauty of Joseon. The Dynasty Cream, the rice-based cleansing balm and the ginseng eye serum are all richer and more nourishing than Cosrx's minimalist textures. Cosrx does have solid hydrators, but its overall aesthetic is closer to a light, watery finish than the plush, layered feel a dry face often prefers.

Sensitive and reactive skin can go either way, but usually does better with the Cosrx Centella line or the Beauty of Joseon Calming line, both of which are almost fragrance-free and built around soothing botanicals. If your skin is easily irritated, our [best cleansers for sensitive skin guide](/blog/best-cleanser-sensitive-skin-gentle/) covers the gentler end of the K-beauty market in more detail.

## Sunscreens: where Beauty of Joseon pulls clearly ahead

Cosrx makes competent sunscreens, but Beauty of Joseon makes some of the best affordable Asian sunscreens on the market in 2026. The Relief Sun Rice and Probiotics SPF 50+ PA++++ is widely praised for its elegant finish, high UVA protection and no white cast on medium to deep skin.

Cosrx has its Aloe Soothing Sun Cream and its Vitamin E Vitalizing Sunscreen, both fine, but neither has broken out the way Beauty of Joseon's Relief Sun has. For everyone except mineral-only users, Beauty of Joseon wins the sunscreen category. Our guide on the [best sunscreens for oily skin](/blog/best-sunscreen-oily-skin-no-white-cast/) has the full framework. The [World Health Organization on ultraviolet radiation](https://www.who.int/news-room/questions-and-answers/item/radiation-the-known-health-effects-of-ultraviolet-radiation) confirms daily broad-spectrum protection is one of the highest-impact steps for long-term skin health.

## How to combine Cosrx and Beauty of Joseon in one routine

You do not have to pick one brand, and most people who have used both for a full year end up mixing them anyway. The most common working setup in 2026 is a Cosrx treatment step layered inside a Beauty of Joseon hydration and sunscreen frame.

A simple example: Beauty of Joseon Radiance Cleansing Balm, any gel cleanser, Cosrx BHA Blackhead Power Liquid two to three nights per week, Cosrx Advanced Snail 96 essence, Beauty of Joseon Glow Serum for the propolis and niacinamide step, Beauty of Joseon Dynasty Cream if you need richer moisture, and Beauty of Joseon Relief Sun every morning.

Order matters more than brand: thinnest to thickest, actives before hydrators, sunscreen last. Our [morning and night routine order guide](/blog/skincare-routine-order-morning-night/) covers the sequencing in detail.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is Cosrx or Beauty of Joseon better for beginners?

Beauty of Joseon is usually the softer landing for a K-beauty beginner. The formulas are gently textured, the fragrance load is low, and the range is small enough that you cannot easily build a bad routine. Cosrx is a strong choice too, but its targeted actives like the 4% BHA can push a beginner into over-exfoliation if they are not careful. Start with Beauty of Joseon hydration and sunscreen, add Cosrx treatments later.

### Which brand is cheaper, Cosrx or Beauty of Joseon?

Cosrx is usually a bit cheaper per product, roughly 15 to 20 percent less on like-for-like buys. But Beauty of Joseon products tend to be more layered, so you often need fewer of them to build a complete routine. For a disciplined shopper, Cosrx wins on price per active. For someone who wants a smaller shelf, Beauty of Joseon can be the cheaper option in practice.

### Can I use Cosrx and Beauty of Joseon together in one routine?

Yes, and most people who use both end up doing exactly that. The formulas are compatible across brands, so a Cosrx BHA followed by a Beauty of Joseon Glow Serum and Relief Sun is a common working setup. The usual caution applies to layering multiple actives, especially acids or retinoids: introduce one product at a time and give your skin two to three weeks to adapt before adding another.

### Which brand is better for acne-prone skin?

Cosrx is usually the stronger acne pick because of the 4% BHA Blackhead Power Liquid and the low fragrance load across the range. Beauty of Joseon's propolis serum is a solid supporting player for oily, blemish-prone skin, but Cosrx has the single most useful treatment product either brand offers for congestion. If your acne is more inflammatory than congestive, our [salicylic acid versus benzoyl peroxide guide](/blog/salicylic-acid-vs-benzoyl-peroxide-acne/) covers where BHA stops being enough.

### Which brand has the better sunscreens?

Beauty of Joseon wins this one clearly. The Relief Sun Rice and Probiotics SPF 50+ is one of the best affordable Asian sunscreens on the market in 2026, with a cosmetically elegant finish and no white cast on most skin tones. Cosrx sunscreens are fine, but they are not category leaders. If sunscreen matters to you, buy the Beauty of Joseon option first.

### Are Cosrx and Beauty of Joseon cruelty-free?

Both brands are widely marketed as cruelty-free in most Western markets. Certifications and regional rules change over time, so verify current status on the official brand pages before buying on that basis. Neither brand is currently sold through the Chinese mainland retail channel that historically triggered animal testing requirements.

## The bottom line

Cosrx is the clinical minimalist. Beauty of Joseon is the hanbang stylist. If you want targeted actives at the lowest price per bottle and you enjoy short, functional ingredient lists, Cosrx is the honest answer. If you want soft-textured formulas built on propolis, rice and ginseng and one of the best affordable sunscreens in the world, Beauty of Joseon earns its small premium.

The most useful thing to know is that this is not really an either-or. Both brands are cheap, both are transparent, and both do a subset of the K-beauty routine better than the other. Buy the Cosrx BHA and the Cosrx snail essence, buy the Beauty of Joseon Glow Serum and the Relief Sun, and you will have covered treatment, hydration and sun protection for less than most people spend on a single luxury cream.
